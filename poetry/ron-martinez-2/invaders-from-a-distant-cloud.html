Invaders from a Distant Cloud

Countless invaders from a distant cloud
quietly carrying out their winter invasion.
How do you small white dots manage to
fall into wondrous inconvenient piles?
Their clever world domination strategy
plays out in parts by cloudy days.
Your curious determination to always fall.
Who gave you such motivation?
Their stubborn resolve brings more of them falling
to replace their shoveled forerunners.
Must your blank persistence remain unmoved
despite already conquering rooftops and lakes?
Their invasion of the ground calmly rages
on through the long hours.
Why must you blanket innocent parked cars
in binding layers of fluffiness and ice?
Their bold hatred of stationary vehicles
must truly be boundless.
You little soft invaders force me to
shovel away your dreams of empire.
I displace these nameless invaders
into mounds that become
cold pillows in the night.
But I’m not fighting them
and this isn't war.
Hopefully, at least some
will simply enjoy
their time as snowflakes
for as long as they can.
Originally written on January 16, 2011
