Under Palest Moonlight

My journey home began quietly enough.
I, peddling silently towards the bike path,
noticed the vibrant moonlight
guiding my way down the street.
I entered the path in anticipation
for I would encounter the wooden bridge
over the river within fleeting moments.
And there it was before me,
stretched out into the distance.
I came to rest above the river’s center.
Leaning on the wooden railing,
I admired the crisp circle of the moon
for it had never been clearer.
The rippling water glistened under
the lights of the nearby bridge
meant for motor vehicles.
A passing ambulance flashed its lights.
The moment was eventually taken
and my ride resumed with a fresh, eerie allure.
Silhouettes of trees danced ahead
in all manner of crisscrossing patterns.
Soon, I was able to leave the trail
to traverse the expanse of a farm field.
There awaited the naked majesty of the sky
whereby the stars were royalty.
Again, I stopped for the view
but it was shorter now since
an oncoming set of bright dots
further down the road
urged me to be on my way.
Emerging from the field,
I came upon the ghostly lit road
leading back to the trail.
Its length was short
but its beauty, transcendent.
Returning to the path,
I focused on completing my ride.
My sentiments were satiated
and the luxury of zoning out
was afforded to me.
Absent of needless worries,
my mind was celebratory
at the lack of such traffic.
It was almost free to,
dare I say, daydream?
A lone flickering light caught my attention.
It was another bike rider, who,
despite their opposite heading,
was a fellow traveler.
As we crossed paths I noted their brisk
yet leisurely pace, the same as mine.
Eventually came the lights of a sports field.
It lit up the trail even more so
since trees were increasingly obscuring the moon.
As I passed alongside it,
the lights were shut off and my path,
save for my trusty headlight,
committed to darkness.
Not long thereafter I disembarked
from the ongoing bike path
to travel through the final set of streets.
Much relaxing downhill riding occurred there.
Even the literal bump in the road
was a welcome sign
of how close I was home.
Soon, I came upon my street
and as I glided down towards the end of it,
I nearly crashed through large piles of leaves
calmly sleeping on top of pavement.
Wouldn’t that have been embarrassing.
I stopped by the mailbox.
Nothing was in it.
I eased in towards the moonlit facade of my house.
Well, the place I rent.
No lights were on.
Soon, I would have to be discerning in my movements
to avoid disturbing those who were sleeping inside.
I parked my bike on the porch and
stepped out onto the walkway.
Looking up, I tried tracing the moon into my memory.
Perhaps I’ll see it again tonight if I dream.
I went indoors to be greeted
by the warm fire of the wood stove.
And this was my journey home.
