Bitter Rose

The last, lovely rose, against an ocean of sand,
with its proud, weakened stance kneeling to the ground,
and its striking petals fading off their dying stem,
with its vibrant, tired colors succumbing to dust,
and amidst the power of the cold, unforgiving wind,
still faithfully smiles up at the sky,
toward the forsaking cloud-covered sun.
To the hopeful rose, rain would soon come,
and the sun would once more shine,
but rain never falls,
and sunlight is never felt,
and this flower diminishes.
How joyous a death it must be,
to be engulfed by the heat of the sun,
or to drown in a barrage of droplets.
But to silently suffocate in neither extreme,
to fade in this dull way,
is the final failure.
So, this lovely flower,
at the mercy of the elements,
cringes to accept its fate,
as indeed it is a bitter, bitter rose…
Originally written on March 11, 2002
Most recently revised on April 21, 2002
