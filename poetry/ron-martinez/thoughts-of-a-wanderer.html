Thoughts of a Wanderer

It is especially rare to find someone
who has a fullness in their personality
that matches of the vividness of their beauty.
Once, long ago, in a quiet, unexpected place,
I met a young woman of such kind.
In her I’ve found all that is worthy of the day and night,
the warmth of her beaming smile,
the serenity in her starry gaze.
She is both proud in stride and
strong in will and in her elegant debonair
I cannot help but be awed in her presence.
There is a fiery lushness to the flow of her hair
and she has a gleaming brightness in the deepness of her eyes.
Her figure is the completion of a sculptor’s dream,
so memorable in form and wordless in perfection.
Her motion is soothing in its naturalness,
yet firmly aware when in action.
She has a merciful ear and
would gladly lend her comforting touch
to those who would need it.
She speaks with the wisest of temperaments
and the calmness of her voice eases the
burdens of any troubled mind.
She is an angel, in both appearance and kindness,
whose joyous laughter can mend even shattered hearts.
She encompasses all the hopes of sunrise
and all the lessons of sunset.
And here I am, through my longest travels,
writing these meek, inadequate words in honor of her.
And in my wandering, my aimless wandering,
I think of how wonderful it would be to be with her.
Originally written on August 31, 2003
Most recently revised on September 5, 2003
