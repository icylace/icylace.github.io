A Voice, A Mic

Oh, how loud I would be,
how voyeuristic my tongue would be,
Had I a microphone in hand
I would deafen every passers-by
With the venom of my diatribe
I would scream and glare
And I’d rant and rave
And I would shout to sing
Oh, how cathartic it would be
In the rapture of the microphone and me
To be rid of shackling silence
And roar with a tempest of pity and wail
Oh, tis a listener I find in thee
And tis a brooding man I be
How I’d sing damned thoughts about damned men
And shriek like the poor entrapped mouse
I’d flail my tongue spitting my every spite
and tumultuous cry trembling the world
And even the deaf and ignorant would heed it
And when their attention is finally under my power,
I would simply…stop
Originally written on July 12, 2002
Most recently revised on July 23, 2002
