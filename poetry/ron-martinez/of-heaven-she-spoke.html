Of Heaven She Spoke

She strayed to the pond’s rippling shore
Golden sways pervaded the leafy chapel
She spoke of heaven
Rustling leaves lead my silence
The horizon gleamed brighter
A whispering light sought redemption
Her fingers scaled the waves
Her reflection shied them away
To the shore’s edge her gaze focused
This moment she thought of heaven,
and I, observing an eternity,
held her wet hand with both of mine
A soft hymn swayed distant trees,
Our horizon softened to a forgiving glow
I gently placed my fingers under her chin
She turned to me, eyes remaining down
I slowly leaned forward
Her eyes quietly rose,
half in shadow, half in orange glow,
meeting mine through perfect clarity
She spoke of heaven,
and I did not reply
I fell sweetly upon her lips,
and we were there.
Originally written on October 13, 2002
Most recently revised on July 23, 2010
