Insanity

Such a gray endless feeling,
such a strange scornful reeling,
to be screaming in fright,
at the blankness of night,
while seeing an abundance of creatures,
flamboyant and without features,
that stalk the houses
and torture the mouses,
swirls in the head,
with dreams of the dead,
that could render the sane,
to leap from window panes.
To have a feeling so removed,
from the containment of the room,
to peer upon a mirror,
to the shock of the seer,
to see the reflection that proved,
what horrors of doom,
permeates the scene,
creeping nearer and nearer,
would set the soul afire,
letting the nightmares conspire,
to dance in the head,
even while asleep in bed.
And to turn, run away,
into the dismal light of the fading day,
then to fall, and then lay,
as sanity sprouts wings and flies away.
Originally written sometime between 2000 and 2001
Most recently revised on November 20, 2002
