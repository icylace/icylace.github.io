The Horizon Forward

a rocky road lies ahead,
and naively do I set forth,
against wind, rain, and thunder,
I face each anew with every step,
the tide of change is yet unbroken,
and further I strive with no curse spoken,
for a tempered will has been my guide,
that untamed fire of survival’s strength
a thickened mist blankets the horizon,
but my sight never could reach as far,
so for my view it does not matter,
my journey ahead may be short-lived
into the uncertain I carry my sighs,
with a soldier’s pride, stride for stride,
should I fall, once more I shall rise,
at first I walk, eventually I crawl
and no kindred soul may lend their hand,
for on this journey I take, I take alone,
no encountered peril can claim my last breath,
for the destination is enough to live for
no pace of sorrow will see me die,
for on this road I travel, I’ve walked before,
and any witness shall bear my head held high,
on this forsaken path I wander
to reach my horizon, I struggle to tread forward,
and tireless still, I solemnly press onward.
Originally written on May 2, 2002
